## Slide 6 — Fundamentals: the “autonomy slider”

**You choose how much the AI drives**

- **Tab** — fast, local-feeling completion for the next edit.
- **Inline / targeted edits** — small, precise changes (think: surgical rewrites).
- **Agent / Composer** — broader tasks: multi-file edits, plans, refactors, tests.
- **Cloud agents** — longer runs on their own machines; you come back to **logs, previews, and reviewable artifacts** — not only diffs.

*Quote (Andrej Karpathy, on site):* The best LLM apps have an **autonomy slider** — you control how much independence to give the AI.

## Slide 7 — Context: why generic chat isn’t enough

**Your repo is the source of truth**

- **Semantic search & indexing** — ask *where* things live and *how* they connect.
- **@ mentions & context** — pull in files, docs, and symbols so the model isn’t guessing.

*Demo tip:* Show one question that needs repo context — e.g. “Where is auth enforced?”

## Slide 8 — From the latest blog: the “third era” (short version)

**Software development is shifting again**

- **Era 1 — Tab:** low-entropy, repetitive work automated keystroke by keystroke.
- **Era 2 — Synchronous agents:** you and the model in a tight prompt loop.
- **Era 3 — Longer, more independent agents:** larger tasks, longer timescales, **less hand-holding** — humans set direction and review **artifacts**.

*Cursor’s bet:* More work moves to **fleets of agents** you equip and review — like a factory for your codebase.

*Read more:* [The third era of AI software development](https://cursor.com/blog/third-era) (Feb 2026)
